# Follow-Up Templates

## Day 3 — Gentle Check-In
**Purpose:** Light touch, remind them of the conversation without being pushy.

**Template Guidelines:**
- Reference the original conversation specifically
- Add a small piece of new value (insight, stat, or resource)
- Keep it under 4 sentences
- End with a soft question, not a hard CTA

**Example:**
> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> Just circling back on my note from earlier this week. I came across [relevant insight/stat] that reminded me of what you mentioned about [their pain point].
>
> Still happy to walk you through how we've helped teams in your space — would a quick 15-minute call be useful?
>
> Best,
> [Agent Name]

---

## Day 5 — Value Drop
**Purpose:** Provide tangible value to re-engage. Make them feel like replying is worth their time.

**Template Guidelines:**
- Lead with value: a case study, a stat, a relevant article, or a quick win
- Tie it directly to their situation or industry
- Keep it helpful, not salesy
- Shorter than Day 3

**Example:**
> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> Thought you might find this interesting — we just helped [similar company/industry] [achieve specific result] in [timeframe].
>
> If that resonates with what you're working on, I'd love to share the details. Either way, hope it's a useful data point.
>
> [Agent Name]

---

## Day 7 — Breakup / Last Touch
**Purpose:** Final attempt. Create urgency through finality, not pressure. Leave the door open gracefully.

**Template Guidelines:**
- Acknowledge you've reached out a few times
- Be direct but not guilt-trippy
- Give them an easy out AND an easy yes
- This is the last automated follow-up

**Example:**
> Hi {{first_name}},
>
> I've reached out a couple of times and I know your inbox is probably overflowing — I'll keep this short.
>
> If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. But if there's even a small chance this could help with [their pain point], I'd love 15 minutes on your calendar.
>
> Either way, I'll stop cluttering your inbox after this. Just reply "yes" if you'd like to chat, or "pass" if now's not the time.
>
> [Agent Name]

---

## General Follow-Up Rules
1. Never copy-paste templates verbatim — always personalize based on the original conversation
2. Reference specific details from their reply (pain points, timeline, team size)
3. Each follow-up should add NEW value, not repeat the same pitch
4. If the lead replied negatively at any point, cancel all follow-ups immediately
5. Match the lead's communication style (formal ↔ casual)
6. Morning sends (9-10 AM in lead's timezone) typically get highest open rates
